How to Close a Credit Card the Right Way

Closing a credit card isn't difficult — but doing it incorrectly can lead to lost rewards, unexpected interest, or negative credit-score effects. This guide explains the safe, structured way to close any card.

Visit Choose.Creditcard

Before You Close, Understand the Consequences

Closing a card reduces your total available credit, which can increase utilisation — one of the bigger factors in most scoring models. However, closing an account with no ongoing use or high fees can still be reasonable.

You should also consider what happens to rewards, remaining balances, and any automatic payments linked to the card.

Step-by-Step: How to Close a Card Safely

Issuers follow strict account-closure procedures. Preparing a few details ahead of time helps the process run smoothly.

  • Pay the balance: Ensure the card is fully paid before closure.
  • Redeem rewards: Points, miles or cashback may disappear after closure.
  • Move recurring payments: Update subscriptions or bills tied to the card.
  • Request closure in writing or via secure message: Keep documentation.
  • Get confirmation: Issuers send a closure letter or secure message.

When Closing a Card Makes Sense

Many cardholders close accounts due to annual fees, lack of benefits, or simplifying their portfolio. Some issuers also allow product changes, which may preserve history while removing unwanted fees.

If the account is older or has a large limit, consider whether keeping it open for score stability might be better despite low usage.

How Closing a Card Can Affect Your Credit Score

Closing a card typically affects eligibility only through utilisation and, indirectly, through reducing the age of your accounts over time. Closed accounts can remain on reports for years, maintaining part of your history.

Score changes vary by person — people with many accounts and high limits may see no meaningful shift at all.

Key Considerations Before You Close

Topic What to Review Why It Matters
Rewards Unused points, miles, cashback Many programs forfeit rewards on closure.
Utilisation Your new total available credit A smaller credit pool can raise your ratio.
Fees Upcoming annual fees Closing before renewal can save money.
Account Age How old the card is Older accounts help long-term credit depth.
Alternatives Product changes, lower tiers May preserve history without paying fees.
